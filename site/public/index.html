Why Ogham?

The name gorm-ogham comes from the Irish gorm, meaning blue, and Ogham (᚛ᚑᚌᚐᚋ᚜), the earliest writing system native to Ireland. Carved into standing stones from the 4th century, Ogham encoded Old Irish along a central stemline using groups of simple strokes.

Like infrastructure as code, Ogham was a system for encoding meaning into structured marks on a surface. The stones were interfaces. The alphabet was the protocol. Fifteen hundred years later, the principle holds: define it clearly, carve it once, and it persists.
